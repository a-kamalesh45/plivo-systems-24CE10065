# NOTES.md

The sender and receiver implement a hybrid reliability protocol combining Forward Error Correction (FEC), selective retransmissions, and deadline-aware playback to minimize deadline misses while staying within the bandwidth budget. Media packets are immediately encapsulated into a custom transport format, and every two consecutive frames generate one XOR parity packet, allowing recovery from a single packet loss without requiring a retransmission. The receiver maintains a small jitter buffer to absorb network jitter, performs FEC reconstruction whenever possible, and requests retransmissions only for packets that cannot be recovered through parity. Feedback uses lightweight ACK and NACK control packets; ACKs allow the sender to garbage-collect transmitted packets while NACKs trigger selective retransmissions from the sender's sliding history. Packet timestamps are exchanged to estimate a smoothed round-trip time (SRTT), although retransmissions remain receiver-driven rather than timeout-driven. Memory usage is bounded through sender and receiver garbage collection, preventing long-running executions from accumulating stale state. The recommended grading configuration is **delay_ms = 44** for **profiles/A.json** and **delay_ms = 96** for **profiles/B.json**, both of which consistently satisfy the assignment constraints of less than 1% deadline misses and less than 2× bandwidth overhead. The protocol performs well under isolated packet losses, moderate jitter, duplication, and limited reordering due to the combination of FEC and selective retransmission. Its primary limitation is burst losses exceeding the recovery capability of the XOR parity groups or retransmissions arriving after the playback deadline, in which case missed frames cannot be recovered before playout.
